1UP Game Rules

Card Facts


  • A pack of cards has 52 playing cards (Excluding the jokers)
  • There are 2 different colour: black and red
  • 4 different suits for each distinct number
  • Each of the suit has a sequence of 13 cards
  • i.e. 2, 3, 4, ……, 10, J, Q, K, ACE

Card Dealings & Order of Play


  • This is a multiplayer game of maximum 4 players and minimum of two
  • At the beginning of the round, each player will get 13 cards
  • The one which possesses the smaller card on the table (2 of space) will get the first move. Or the smallest of the cards available in players’ hand if there are fewer players than 4.
  • The play will happen clockwise to begin with.
  • The next player can decide to play or skip


Moves


  • The game is previous-move dependent.
  • The next move has to replicate a similar pattern to the previous, which works using the power scaling.
  • There are a number of possible moves that could be made.
  • 1 card, so the next move will also have to be 1 card but with a bigger value.
  • A pair of black or red, the response will be a higher value pair or a pair of red to beat black.
  • 3 cards or four cards of the same number. You need a bigger number to beat this.
  • A sequence of at least 3 consecutive cards, of the same suit. To beat this, you need a bigger value sequence. Note: They must be of the same number of cards, but could be bigger value of the same suit, or a higher value suit (minimum of the same value cards or higher), e.g. Hearts to beat Diamonds.
  • A sequence of at least 3 consecutive pairs. You need a higher value sequence to beat this or red sequence to beat black sequence (minimum of the same value cards or higher)

Rounds and change of events


  • The round will proceed clockwise.
  • The next player can choose to play or skip (save cards for another round). Note: If you skip this turn, you cannot join back in until the round ends.
  • The round will end when a player plays his/her hand but there is no response from the rest of the player.
  • The player that win the last round can now initiate a new sequence of play. And the game carry on.


Winner ranks


  • A player finishes when he or she got rid of all his/ her cards, the rest must play on until there is only one player left.
  • After one player finishes his/her game, if no player can beat the current move or choose to skip, the next player can initiate a new round.
  • The orders of winner ranks depend on their finishing positions.
  • i.e. first, second, third and last
  • At the end, a set of determined prizes or punishments will be giving to players that finish in certain positions.



Additional rules


  • Automatic win if you have a sequence of 13 cards of the same suit.
  • 6 pairs of an increasing sequence.

Assistant power cards


  • We will decide 4 special power card to disrupt the flow of the game, each player will get a random card at the start of the game.
  • Peek at a chosen card in a player’s hand
  • Drop one card from your stack.
  • Change the order of play, anticlockwise after your turn.
  • Stop another person move for one turn
  • At the beginning of your turn, you can roll two dices, if the dices match, you could activate your power card.
